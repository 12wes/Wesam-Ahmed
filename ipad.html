Ipad

On January 27, 2010, Apple introduced their much-anticipated media tablet, the iPad.[306][307] It offers multi-touch interaction with multimedia formats including newspapers, e-books, photos, videos, music, word processing documents, video games, and most existing iPhone apps using a 9.7-inch screen.[308] It also includes a mobile version of Safari for web browsing, as well as access to the App Store, iTunes Library, iBookstore, Contacts, and Notes. Content is downloadable via Wi-Fi and optional 3G service or synced through the user's computer.[309] AT&T was initially the sole U.S. provider of 3G wireless access for the iPad.

On September 9, 2015, Apple announced the iPad Pro, an iPad with a 12.9-inch display that supports two new accessories, the Smart Keyboard and Apple Pencil.[318] An updated IPad Mini 4 was announced at the same time.[319] A 9.7-inch iPad Pro was announced on March 21, 2016.[320] On June 5, 2017.
